Intro to Soccer

Get Involved

Do you love soccer and want to know how you can get involved with the sport? There are a few easy ways to make an impact and have fun! Here you'll discover how you can potentially make soccer into a part-time job.

Coaching

The easiest way to get involved with soccer in your community is to coach a team. While coaching is a tough and time-consuming task, it can also be a fun experience. Coaches are essential to the success of a team, and if you want to help younger players shine, then becomming one might just be the perfect option for you!

Becomming a Coach

If you want to coach a team, you must first contact your local soccer club to find out if there are any open positions. If the club has a team without a coach, you may be asked to fill in this opening. But, if you know a group of potential players, you may also request to be their coach.

Improving your Coaching

Much like being a player, coaching requires a specific set of skills too. Coaches need to be patient with their players, as they are the ones teaching them how to play and how to improve their skils.

Refereeing

Refereeing is a good way to get more involved with soccer in your town if you want to make an extra buck. This sounds exciting, and it is, but it's important to understand the responsibility that comes with being a referee. Referees hold the authrity over game and must fairly enforce the rules of the game. It is a lot of work to be a good ref, but it's a good job for soccer lovers.

If you want to become a referee, you may discover useful resources on the US Soccer website.

Put shortly, there are 4 steps to becomming a referee.

  1. Contact your local soccer board. Ask if your town needs more referees and look for opportunities elsewhere if not. This is important, because you want to be able to put your skills to work once you complete the requirements to become a ref.
  2. Sign up for a class. If you want to become a referee, you'll have to get certified. This certification ensures that you have complete knowledge of the rules of soccer, and shows coaches that you are qualified to be refereeing their games.
  3. Once you pass your test, you'll have to acquire the proper equipment. This can also be found on the US Soccer website. This equipment includes a proper uniform and items that will help you maintain authority over the game.
  4. You may want to become a higher grade referee if you feel as though you have the right skills. To do this, you'll have to take additional classes for each level you wish to advance to.
