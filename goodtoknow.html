Good to Know

Read through this page to discover additional information that will help your team be more successful.

Positions

Offense

In most situations, offensive players are the ones who score the goals for their team. As exciting, and perhaps even simple, as this may sound, there are actually many skills required to be a successful attacker.

  • Advanced levels of ball control
  • Good understanding of proper positioning
  • Powerful shot

Advanced levels of ball control are what really set great offensive players from good ones. Great offensive players are able to make the ball do their bidding, giving them an advantage against opposing defenders. In addition, ball control helps the offensive player keep the ball in his or her team's possesion.

It is important for all players to understand proper positioning, but positioning is especially useful to offenive players. When attempting to score a goal, fowards need to work together to get around the defense and goalkeeper. Positioning allows them to know where on the field they will be most useful to the other fowards in their attempt to score a goal.

The most obvious, and arguably most imprtant skill that a foward must have is a powerful shot. Since these players are usually the ones scoring the goals for the team, they must have the ability to do so. The stronger a player's shot, the more difficult it is for the goalkeeper to make a save, making them more likely to score.

Midfield

Midfield is another one of the standard positions in soccer. A midfielder's job is to bridge the gap between the offense and defense. This means that players in this position must pull a lot of weight in order for their team to be successful. Some of the most important characteristics of a good midfielder are as follows.

  • Ball control
  • Passing abilities
  • Stamina

Just like attackers, midfielders also need to have decent ball control in order to effectively move the ball from the defense to the offense. Good ball control helps with being able to deliver accurate passes to teammates all over the field. Accurate and unpredicatble passes allow midfielders to control the field and keep the ball in their team's posession.

Because midfielders have to bring the ball from the defense to the attackers, they are required to do a whole lot of running. This means that in order to be a successful midfielder, you have to have killer stamina.

Defense

Defense is arguably the most important position in soccer. Many amature coaches make the mistake of putting all of their "best" players on attack. However, if you think about it, defending your own goal is more important than scoring them. You can still tie a game (meaining you didn't win or lose) if your team doesn't score any goals (0 - 0). However, if your goal is left undefended or badly defended, you are more likely to lose, as it is harder to score goals than prevent them. This being said, it is important to understand that a solid defense is crucial to a winning team.

As with any other position, being a defender requires specialized skills.

  • Ball control
  • Powerful kick
  • Understanding of positioning

Every position requires some level of ball control, but defenders require a different execution of the same skills that an attacker or midfielder would use. Defenders need to withstand the jukes of an opposing attacker and know when it is safe to stoop in and steal the ball.

Above all positions, it is important for defenders to understand the importance of proper positioning, especially when it comes to defending an attack. Opposing offensive players are likely to be passing the ball in order to keep your defense guessing, so it is imporant to understand where you should be on the field in order to successfully block an attack. If players bunch up or move out of position, an opportunity to breach the defences may appear.

Goalkeeper

There is one exception to the no-hands rule. Goalkeepers are the teams' last defense when it comes to protecting the goal from an attacker's attempt to score a goal, which would allow them to gain a point for their team. This is what makes the goalkeeper so important.
